I’m someone who fell in love with video games as a kid and never really grew out of it. What started with games like GTA: Vice City turned into a real curiosity about how games actually work, especially the low-level stuff like rendering, shaders, and engine systems. Even though I’m studying Industrial Engineering, I’ve gradually shifted my focus toward computer science by working as a TA in algorithm design and gamification, and by getting hands-on with internships and projects in game studios. I’ve built several projects using Unreal Engine, Unity, and OpenGL, diving into gameplay systems, optimization, and core system development, while also getting more and more into the artistic side of game design, like world-building and visual storytelling. I love the idea of mixing code and creativity, and I'm always looking for ways to turn that mix into something players can truly feel and connect with.
